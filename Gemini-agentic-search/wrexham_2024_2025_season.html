Wrexham AFC 2024-2025 Season Analysis

Domestic performance

League One 2024-2025

Wrexham AFC competed in League One, the third tier of English football, during the 2024-2025 season. They achieved a remarkable second-place finish with a record of 27 wins, 11 draws, and 8 losses, accumulating 92 points. This performance secured their promotion to the EFL Championship, marking the first time in English football history that a team has achieved three consecutive promotions within the top five divisions.

Best League Results: Wrexham secured several significant victories, including 3-0 wins against Reading (August 24, 2024), Shrewsbury Town (September 7, 2024), Exeter City (November 23, 2024), Burton Albion (April 5, 2025), and Charlton Athletic (April 26, 2025). The 3-0 victory over Charlton Athletic notably sealed their promotion. They also had a strong 4-1 win against Northampton Town on October 5, 2024.

Worst League Results: Their biggest defeats in the league included a 2-4 loss to Sheffield United (August 13, 2024) and a 1-3 loss to Birmingham City (September 16, 2024).

Cup Competitions

FA Cup: Wrexham AFC exited the FA Cup in the First Round proper, losing to Harrogate Town FC.

EFL Cup (Carabao Cup): The team's journey in the EFL Cup was also brief, as they lost in the First Round to Sheffield United.

EFL Trophy: Wrexham had a notable run in the EFL Trophy, reaching the semi-finals of the competition.

Team notes

Key Players

The standout player for the 2024-2025 season was Ollie Rathbone, who was crowned Wrexham AFC's Player of the Season. Other significant contributors to the team's success included Elliot Lee, who was the top goalscorer across all competitions with 9 goals, and also scored 8 in the league alongside Steven Fletcher and Ollie Rathbone. Ryan Barnett was a key creative force, contributing 9 assists. Other notable players for their consistent performance included Max Cleworth, James McClean, George Dobson, and Sam Smith.

Team and Manager Changes

Manager Phil Parkinson continued to lead Wrexham AFC throughout the 2024-2025 season, guiding them to their historic third consecutive promotion.

The club made several significant transfer movements both before and during the season to strengthen the squad for their League One campaign and subsequent promotion to the Championship. Notable arrivals included Liberato Cacace (£2.2m from Empoli), George Thomason (£1.2m from Bolton), Ryan Hardie (£700k from Plymouth), Danny Ward (free from Leicester), Lewis O'Brien (£3m from Nottingham Forest), Conor Coady (£2m from Leicester), Kieffer Moore (£2m from Sheffield United), Josh Windass (free from Sheffield Wednesday), Nathan Broadhead (£7.5m from Ipswich), and Callum Doyle (£5m from Manchester City). In January 2025, Wrexham also broke their club-record transfer fee to sign Sam Smith from Reading for $1.2 million, and former Premier League striker Jay Rodriguez also joined.

Key departures included Steven Fletcher, Mark Howard, Josh Adam, Jordan Davies, Harry Dean, Brad Foster, and Liam Hall, who were released at the end of their contracts. Sam Dalby declined a new contract offer. Other players who left or were loaned out included Will Boyle, Luke McNicholas, Luke Bolton, Paul Mullin, Sebastien Revan, Mo Faal, Jake Bickerstaff, Jack Marriott, Callum Edwards, George Evans, Ollie Palmer, Thomas O'Connor, and Jacob Mendy.

Notable events

The 2024-2025 season for Wrexham AFC continued to be a subject of intense public and media interest, with their journey chronicled in the fourth season of the documentary series 'Welcome to Wrexham'.

The club's rapid ascent through the English football leagues, fueled by significant investment from owners Ryan Reynolds and Rob McElhenney, continued to draw attention. While celebrated by fans, this rise also generated discussions within the broader 'old school' football community, with some expressing reservations about teams 'buying their way' through divisions.

A notable incident during the season involved player Ryan Longman, who reportedly suffered abuse from a segment of the club's own fanbase. This event highlighted a broader concern within UK football regarding the increasing hostility and abuse directed towards players, often exacerbated by social media.
